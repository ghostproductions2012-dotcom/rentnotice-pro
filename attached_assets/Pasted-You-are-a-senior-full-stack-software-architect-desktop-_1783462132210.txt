You are a senior full-stack software architect, desktop app engineer, UI/UX designer, legal-document workflow engineer, and corporate compliance systems designer.

Build a professional standalone desktop software application for property management companies called:

RentNotice Pro — California 3-Day Pay or Quit Notice Tool

The application must help property management staff import tenant ledger data, identify rent-only balances, calculate unpaid rent by month, review exclusions, and generate California 3-Day Notices to Pay Rent or Quit with supporting service/proof documents.

This software must be built in Replit, with final installer builds handled through GitHub.

The software must be compatible with GPT, Claude/Fable 5, and Gemini coding workflows. Code must be clean, modular, non-redundant, maintainable, and production-ready.

Do not create conflicting duplicate functions. Do not hard-code fragile assumptions. Use clear separation between UI, data parsing, calculation logic, document generation, validation, storage, audit logs, and export logic.

This is legal-adjacent software. The application must include strong disclaimers that it does not provide legal advice and that notice templates should be reviewed by a qualified California landlord-tenant attorney before production use.

The application should never silently include fees or non-rent charges in the notice amount.

⸻

1. Product Objective

Create downloadable desktop software for California property management companies that prepares 3-Day Notices to Pay Rent or Quit by:

1. Importing tenant ledgers.
2. Detecting rent charges, payments, partial payments, credits, reversals, and non-rent charges.
3. Calculating rent-only balances month by month.
4. Excluding all non-rent balances.
5. Allowing staff review and override.
6. Generating:
    * California 3-Day Notice to Pay Rent or Quit
    * Proof of service
    * Posting/mailing checklist
    * Internal calculation review page
    * Audit summary
    * Optional attached ledger backup
7. Exporting a final PDF notice package.
8. Tracking notice status from draft through attorney handoff.

⸻

6. Calculation Logic

Calculation must be transparent and auditable.

Formula:

Monthly rent owed = rent charges for month − payments applied to rent − credits applied to rent

Rules:

1. Rent period must always begin on the 1st day of the month.
2. Rent period must always end on the last day of that same month.
3. Detect the final day of each month automatically.
4. Handle February and leap years.
5. Multiple unpaid months must be listed separately.
6. Partial payments must reduce the balance.
7. Multiple payments in one month must be recognized.
8. Credits must reduce the balance only when clearly rent-related.
9. Overpayments may carry forward only if the ledger clearly supports it.
10. Security deposits must not be applied unless manually authorized and marked for legal review.
11. Ambiguous payments must be flagged.
12. Payments received but not clearly applied must trigger a warning.
13. Non-rent charges must never be included by default.
14. Manual overrides must require a reason.

The calculation screen must show:

* Month
* Rent period
* Rent charged
* Payments received
* Credits applied
* Excluded charges
* Rent-only balance
* Warnings
* Final selected amount

Example:

July 1, 2026 – July 31, 2026
Rent charged: $2,500
Payments applied: $1,000
Credits: $0
Excluded non-rent charges: $125 late fee, $85 utility charge
Rent-only balance: $1,500

7. Date Requirements

Every selected rent month must use:

Start date: first day of month
End date: final day of same month

Examples:

* July 1, 2026 – July 31, 2026
* February 1, 2028 – February 29, 2028
* February 1, 2027 – February 28, 2027

If the user attempts to select a custom date range that does not begin on the 1st and end on the final day of the month, show a blocking warning.

The software may allow advanced override only with supervisor permission and audit trail logging.
